About Me

I'm a student at the University of Georgia majoring in Computer Science. I started programming in 5th grade as a game developer in ROBLOX. My first programming language was a modified version of Lua, and I tried to make different games to challenge my ability to code. It was always exciting when I could run a game and everything worked as I intended. I eventually left the game development and programming community and thought about studying to be an Aerospace Engineer. I took a few dual enrollment courses including Calculus 2 and Principles of Physics and had an unexpected awakening of what lied ahead. Once I started college, I took my first Computer Science class and realized that, although different than when I first started, I still liked programming and I still had the same excitement that I did before.

While I enjoy learning programming languages like Python and Java, I also studied Spanish for 6 years and Italian for 3 years and love practicing to retain my knowledge. I practice Spanish daily at Chick-fil-A when I talk with my Hispanic co-workers or take orders for people that may struggle with English. After Spanish, I had always wanted to learn Italian because I have Italian ancestors, so I took the opportunity to learn it at UGA. I got the chance to test my knowledge when my family and I traveled to Italy as a high-school graduation trip. It was an amazing experience, not only to see different parts of Italy but to be able to travel and communicate without depending on English.

The most recent hobby I've taken on is golf. My fiancee played competitively for many years, so I thought I would learn to play with her and her family. It was frustrating trying to get the motions down but once I started to get the hang of it, golf turned out to be a fun weekend activity.
